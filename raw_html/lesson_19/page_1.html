Marketing: Sharing your website with the world – Tell a story!


STEP 1 :

Who is your target audience?

Remember: User Personas


STEP 2 :

What is your messaging?

How are you communicating your product?



STEP 2a :

Develop a messaging framework


Example Messaging Framework

  • First impression: When a user first lands on our social media platform, we want them to feel excited and eager to try our product.

  • Value Statement: After spending a few minutes on our site, the user should feel excited because they found a company that promotes a healthy lifestyle and provides a healthy snack that is promoted through engaging and fun content.

  • Proof: Our content demonstrates that we value a healthy lifestyle and want to provide consumers with a snack that satisfies their taste buds but also provides a great user experience.


STEP 2b :

Develop a core strategy statement


A core content strategy statement is…


a concise statement (usually fewer than 30 words ) that summarizes choices about…

  1. why a company (or team) produces content

  2. for whom

  3. how it manifests (the “what”).


Example core strategy statements:

  1. The content we produce helps Origin Meals increase new orders by 10% and recurring orders by 20% by providing motivating and informative content that makes busy single athletes feel excited or confident so that they can fit paleo eating into their lifestyles.

  2. Curate visually appealing content that promotes healthy snacking and a healthy lifestyle. In addition to this, inform snackers about Mix N Munch, and encourage them to purchase our trail mix.


Here’s a sample template to use for a core strategy statement:


The content we produce helps our company accomplish (Goal #1) and (Goal #2) by providing (adjective) and (adjective) content that makes (audience description) feel (a type of emotion) or (a type of emotion) so that they can (task) or (task) .
