Datatypes Review

There are four kinds of datatypes in Python: integers, floats, strings, and boolean. These four datatypes will be used often during your journey with Python.

An integer (int) is a whole number. No decimal points are allowed for integers (12, not 12.0). They can be used for counting and calculation.

A float is a decimal value. This can be whole numbers (written as 12.0 for example), but can include any decimal number. These are used for calculations.

A string (str) is a sequence of characters. They must be written in quotation marks. Examples of strings include \"Hello world!\", \"10\", and \" \".

A boolean (bool) only consists of True or False values. The T and F in True and False must be capitalized to represent booleans.

Math in Pythonï»¿

With the datatypes integer and float, we can do math! We can do the basic math: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. We can also do exponentiation.

There are three different kinds of division, and the differences are important to point out. There is regular division, notated by one /. This will give you either a whole integer or a decimal,

depending on the problem. Next, we have integer division, notated with //. Integer division will only give you the whole integer of the problem. For example, when you divide 12 by 7, you

get 1 remainder 5. With integer division, only the 1 would be showcased. Finally, we have modulo, or remainder division, notated with the % symbol. Similar to integer division, this will only

give you part of a value. Instead of the whole integer, we get the remainder of the problem. If we take 12 divided by 7 again, if we did modulo division, Python would print out 5, as that is

the remainder value.
