Model:

Model is the logic related to the data in your website. It interacts with the database.

Ex: for Netflix, the model includes the definitions of movie, user, series


View:

View is the “ frontend, ” or the logic and elements associated with the UI (User Interface). This is what the customer sees/how the customer interacts when using your website.


Controller:

Controller is the link/connection between the View and the Model (the middle man). It mainly manipulates data using the Model and interacts with the Views to show/display the final output .


MVC can be implemented differently depending on the programming language and framework you are using!

Why is MVC important?

Let’s take a look at a Real Life MVC Example:


Let’s assume you go to a restaurant,

You will not go to the kitchen and prepare food - which you can surely do at your home. Instead, you just go there and wait for the waiter to come on.


Now the waiter comes to you, and you just order the food.

The waiter doesn't know who you are and what you want, he just writes down the details of your food order.


Then, the waiter moves to the kitchen.

In the kitchen, the waiter is not the person to prepare your food.


The cook prepares your food.

The waiter has given your order to him along with your table number.


The cook then prepares the food for you.

He uses ingredients to cook the food. Let's assume that you order a burger. So, he needs the bun, tomato, onion, cheese, etc...

Which he gets from the refrigerator!


The cook finally hands back the food to the waiter.

Now it is the job of the waiter to move this food outside the kitchen and to your table.
